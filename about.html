Why we exist

Supply chain work begins long before freight is booked.

Managing a China supply chain involves more than moving cargo. Suppliers need clear requirements. Samples and client decisions must be tracked. Agreed quality and packaging checks may be needed before shipment. Compatible goods from different factories may need coordinated receipt, consolidation, and separately agreed shipment preparation. Documentation, transport, customs responsibilities, and the confirmed destination scope all have to connect.

Jinghang Global Supply Chain exists to coordinate those moving parts.

We work with hardware startups, small and mid-sized businesses, and product brands that need practical execution in China but do not have a full local procurement and logistics team.

Depending on the project, we can support supplier search, sample and purchasing coordination, quality inspection coordination, consolidation, international logistics, special-cargo review, and partner-warehouse or fulfillment-related coordination.

Our execution model is collaboration-based. Jinghang's own team and third-party service providers are configured flexibly according to each project's needs.

Our role is to create clearer responsibilities between the client, suppliers, inspection providers, partner facilities, logistics providers, and delivery partners—and to surface important exceptions for the client's decision.

Our coordinating role

  • Translate the project brief into practical supplier and operating requirements.
  • Connect samples, agreed quality checks, packaging requirements, and confirmed cargo preparation.
  • Coordinate the handoff from Chinese suppliers to international delivery.
  • Keep responsibilities, milestones, risks, and exceptions visible.
  • Support a more repeatable supply chain across future orders.

More than freight forwarding

International transport is one part of the supply chain—not the whole supply chain.

We review what happens before cargo is ready, what preparation is included for movement, and which destination responsibilities are confirmed for the shipment.

How we think

Practical operating principles for every project.

Good coordination comes from clear responsibilities, early visibility, and communication that helps the client make decisions.

01

Clarity Before Action

Define the product, scope, responsibilities, documents, milestones, and decision points before execution begins.

02

Early Visibility

Surface missing information, quality concerns, supplier delays, packaging issues, and logistics constraints early.

03

Practical Communication

Explain what happened, what it affects, what options exist, and what requires approval.

04

Compliance Over Shortcuts

We do not support inaccurate declarations, counterfeit goods, duty avoidance, or other non-compliant practices.

05

Long-Term Operating Value

The objective is not merely to complete one shipment. It is to help make the next order clearer and more repeatable.
