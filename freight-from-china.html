Freight From China: Express, Air, or Sea?

The right freight method depends on the product, packed dimensions and weight, origin, destination country and postcode, target timing, customs requirements, and the total scope of the quotation.

The final mode, route, provider, acceptance conditions, responsibilities, included charges, and exclusions are confirmed in writing for the shipment.

Compare the feasible options

Choose against shipment facts, not a generic fastest-or-cheapest claim.

A smaller shipment is not automatically best by express, and a larger shipment is not automatically best by sea. No fixed weight, time, price, or acceptance threshold applies to every project.

01

Express or courier

May be considered when the cargo is acceptable to the relevant express network. Review product restrictions, chargeable weight, package limits, postcode coverage, customs process, service level, and destination scope.

02

Air freight

May be considered when the cargo meets applicable air-handling and document requirements. Review packed volume and weight, local handling, pickup, final delivery, security, customs, and total scope.

03

Sea freight

May be considered when cargo, packaging, origin port, destination, delivery arrangement, and project timing are feasible. Review consolidation or container arrangements, port handling, customs, and final delivery.

01 · Prepare

Accurate cargo and destination details make the comparison usable.

Missing or changed shipment facts can change feasibility, timing, and price.

Information to send

  • Pickup city or supplier location, number of pickup points, readiness date, and consolidation request
  • Clear product description, intended use, quantity, SKU mix, and carton count
  • Packed carton or crate dimensions, gross weight, cargo value, and available packing list
  • Photos, specifications, and available compliance documents where relevant
  • Batteries, liquids, powders, magnets, branded goods, oversized dimensions, unusual handling, or other regulated characteristics
  • Destination country, postcode, exact delivery point, target timing, preferred mode, and requested pickup, terminal, DAP, or DDP scope
Input boundary Final information and documents depend on the product and route. There is no universal battery-document list, oversized threshold, or one-form-fits-all customs checklist.

02 · Review

Prepare a route recommendation in five steps.

A recommendation is not final until the written scope is accepted.

  1. Confirm product, packaging, dimensions, weight, value, special characteristics, and documents.
  2. Review supplier locations, readiness dates, collection points, compatibility, and consolidation needs.
  3. Confirm destination postcode, named place, customs needs, service scope, and provider conditions.
  4. Compare feasible express, air, and sea options for timing, handling, customs, responsibilities, and total quoted scope.
  5. Issue a written quotation or request missing information, with assumptions, inclusions, exclusions, and adjustment conditions.

03 · Consolidate

Coordinate compatible goods before international dispatch.

Multi-supplier consolidation is reviewed against timing, packaging, cargo compatibility, and release conditions.

Jinghang can assess collection and consolidation for goods from multiple Chinese suppliers. The review covers supplier locations, ready dates, cartons, cargo compatibility, packaging condition, destination, customs requirements, and client release instructions.

Scope boundary Receiving checks, sorting, labeling, repacking, palletizing, storage, and other preparation work are not universal inclusions. Work, materials, evidence, execution party, storage period, fee, and release rule must be feasible and agreed in writing. Consolidation does not automatically reduce total cost, eliminate damage, or remove customs requirements.

04 · Confirm coverage

Review actual origin, cargo, postcode, and delivery scope.

Route examples are not universal guarantees.

Confirmed route examples

  • China to the United States, Europe, Hong Kong, and selected Southeast Asian markets
  • United States to Europe, Hong Kong, and Mainland China
  • Europe to the United States, Hong Kong, and Mainland China
  • Other feasible routes reviewed case by case

Exact airport, port, pickup, final delivery, remote-area, postcode, and door scope remain shipment-specific. Route, provider, customs, and destination-delivery feasibility are confirmed only after review.

05 · Allocate responsibility

Use DDP or DAP with an exact named place.

DDU is a legacy term and should be clarified rather than used as a current primary service term.

DDP may be available for the United States and 25 verified European countries, subject to shipment review. DDP and DAP for Canada and Australia require actual-shipment feasibility and route review. DAP may be arranged globally, subject to cargo, route, customs, destination, and partner review.

06 · Review exceptions

Special cargo is reviewed before acceptance or routing.

There is no universal document list or fixed oversized or overweight threshold.

Battery-related, sensitive, oversized, overweight, and other regulated cargo may be assessed case by case. Acceptance depends on cargo characteristics, documents, packaging, origin, destination, handling, provider rules, customs requirements, and route feasibility.

Cargo not handled

  • Prohibited goods
  • Products restricted or banned from export by China
  • Products restricted or banned from import by the destination country
  • Counterfeit or intellectual-property-infringing goods
  • Cargo without required compliance documents

07 · Confirm in writing

Read the scope and exclusions, not only the headline amount.

The written quotation defines the operational and commercial boundary.

The quotation should identify

  • Proposed mode or service, origin, destination, postcode, and named delivery point
  • Pickup, consolidation, terminal, port, airport, customs, and final-delivery scope where applicable
  • Shipment data and chargeable-weight or measurement assumptions
  • Service term, responsibilities, included charges, exclusions, and quote validity
  • Special-cargo, packaging, document, or acceptance conditions
  • Whether tracking, updates, proof of delivery, insurance, claims, storage, redelivery, or remote-area charges apply
No universal defaults Tracking, proof of delivery, update frequency, insurance, claims, storage, redelivery, and remote-area treatment are route- and provider-specific and must be confirmed when relevant.

Direct answers

Freight from China questions

Answers depend on the actual cargo, route, postcode, provider, customs, and written scope.

Should I use express, air freight, or sea freight from China?

The suitable option depends on the product, packed dimensions and gross weight, destination postcode, target timing, customs requirements, and total quoted scope. Jinghang compares feasible options after reviewing the actual shipment; there is no universal mode or fixed weight threshold for every project.

What information is needed for an accurate freight quotation?

Provide the origin, destination country and postcode, product description, quantity, carton count, packed dimensions, gross weight, cargo value, target timing, preferred mode, supplier readiness, and all battery, liquid, powder, magnet, branded, oversized, or regulated characteristics. Photos, packing lists, specifications, and available compliance documents may also be required.

Can you consolidate goods from several Chinese suppliers?

Yes, compatible goods may be collected and consolidated after supplier timing, packaging, cargo characteristics, destination, customs requirements, and release conditions have been reviewed. Receiving checks, repacking, labeling, storage, and other preparation work are included only when agreed in writing.

Can you ship products containing batteries?

Battery-related cargo may be assessed case by case. Acceptance depends on product and battery characteristics, documents, packaging, origin, destination, provider rules, customs requirements, and route feasibility. There is no universal document list or guaranteed acceptance.

Can you handle sensitive, regulated, oversized, or overweight cargo?

Legally shippable cargo in these categories may be reviewed case by case. Exact dimensions, weight, product, handling needs, documents, route, destination, and partner conditions determine whether a workable option is available.

Do you provide door-to-door delivery?

The exact pickup and final-delivery scope is shipment-specific. The written quotation must state the named destination, postcode, door, port, airport, terminal, and other handoff responsibilities that apply. Door delivery should not be assumed unless confirmed.

Can you arrange DDP or DAP shipping?

DDP may be available for the United States and 25 verified European countries subject to shipment review. Canada and Australia require actual-shipment feasibility and route review for DDP or DAP. DAP may be arranged globally, subject to review. Every quotation must state the term, named place, responsibilities, inclusions, and exclusions.

Can you guarantee transit time or customs clearance?

No. Timing depends on cargo readiness, provider schedules, route conditions, customs, inspections, and destination delivery. Jinghang does not make a universal transit, clearance, or delivery guarantee; any shipment-specific commitment must be recorded in writing.

Will I receive tracking or proof of delivery?

Tracking, shipment-update cadence, and proof-of-delivery availability depend on the route, provider, and agreed service. These items must be confirmed in the quotation or shipment instructions and are not promised as universal defaults.

Is cargo insurance included?

Insurance is not automatically included. Availability, coverage, exclusions, declared value, evidence, claim procedure, responsibilities, and fees must be confirmed in writing for the shipment.
