China sourcing

Finding and comparing suppliers

How do I find a reliable supplier in China?

Start with a clear product specification, expected quantity, quality standard, compliance needs, and target market. We can identify potential suppliers, organize comparable information, coordinate samples, and flag gaps, while final approval should combine commercial, technical, legal, and quality checks appropriate to your product.

What information do you need before searching for a factory?

Please provide drawings or specifications, intended use, materials, key tolerances, expected quantity, packaging needs, destination market, and any required testing or certification. Clear requirements reduce unsuitable matches and make supplier responses easier to compare.

Can you verify whether a supplier is a real factory?

We can coordinate document review, business-information checks, video calls, or an on-site assessment where appropriate. These checks reduce uncertainty but do not replace legal due diligence, financial review, or a client’s final supplier approval.

How do you compare potential suppliers?

We compare the factors agreed for the project, such as manufacturing fit, specification response, sample quality, communication, production controls, packaging, documentation, and commercial terms. We present material differences rather than selecting a supplier solely on the lowest quotation.

Can you source both standard and custom-made products?

Potentially. Standard products usually require confirmation of the exact model and packaging, while custom products need clearer drawings, materials, tolerances, tooling, intellectual-property controls, and approval criteria. Feasibility is assessed from the product brief.

Can you help if I have only a product idea?

We can help turn an initial idea into a supplier-ready information list and identify what is still missing. Product design, engineering validation, patent advice, and regulatory approval may require qualified specialists before factory outreach is productive.

Supplier coordination

Managing communication and follow-up

Can you coordinate suppliers I already found?

Yes. With your authorization, we can communicate requirements, follow up on open items, coordinate samples and inspections, and connect cargo handoffs. You retain control of supplier approval and major commercial decisions unless the written scope states otherwise.

Can you act as our China-side point of contact?

Yes, for the activities defined in the project scope. We can centralize supplier updates, quality findings, cargo readiness, and logistics handoffs so your team has a clearer China-side operating contact.

How do you keep supplier instructions clear?

We work from written requirements, confirm important changes, track unresolved questions, and connect approvals to identifiable samples or documents. Clients should name the people authorized to approve specifications, exceptions, and release decisions.

Can you coordinate several suppliers for one product or shipment?

Yes. We can maintain a supplier and milestone list, coordinate compatible delivery windows, receive goods, and organize inspection or consolidation steps. Each supplier’s responsibilities and the dependencies between parts should be defined early.

Do you negotiate with factories?

We can communicate target requirements and discuss quotations, minimum quantities, packaging, payment milestones, or corrective actions within an agreed mandate. We do not promise a particular concession, and the client approves final commercial terms.

What happens when a supplier reports a delay or specification change?

We document the change, identify affected milestones or handoffs, request supporting details, and present practical options for the client’s decision. Production or shipment should not proceed on a material change without the required approval.

Sampling & procurement

From sample requests to order follow-up

Can you support product sampling?

Yes. We can coordinate sample requests, confirm the version and requested attributes, arrange receipt, and record supplier feedback. The client should define what the sample must demonstrate and who has authority to approve it.

Can you consolidate samples from multiple factories?

Yes, where the samples can be accepted and shipped together. We can receive, identify, photograph, and combine them, subject to product restrictions, packing needs, and the chosen delivery service.

Does an approved sample guarantee that production will match it?

No. An approved sample is a useful reference, but production controls, materials, tolerances, and inspection criteria must also be documented. A retained or clearly identified reference sample can support later comparison.

Can you help coordinate purchase orders?

We can coordinate approved order details, supplier acknowledgements, milestone follow-up, and document collection. The contracting party, payment responsibilities, ownership of goods, and approval authority must be agreed before purchasing activity begins.

Can you pay suppliers on our behalf?

Any payment-handling arrangement requires a separate written review of the parties, currency, invoicing, ownership, compliance, and supporting records. Do not send funds or assume an agency payment structure until it has been expressly confirmed.

How do you follow production milestones?

We can track agreed events such as material confirmation, sample approval, production status, inspection readiness, packing completion, and cargo release. Supplier updates are recorded as reported and may require independent verification when the risk justifies it.

Quality control

Checks, evidence, and corrective action

What types of inspections can you arrange?

Depending on the product and risk, we can coordinate pre-production checks, in-process checks, pre-shipment inspection, loading checks, or targeted verification. The method, sample size, checklist, access, and acceptance criteria must be agreed in advance.

How is an inspection checklist created?

The checklist should come from the approved specification, reference sample, purchase requirements, packaging rules, known failure modes, and destination needs. We can help organize it, but the client or its qualified technical representative should approve the criteria.

Can you provide photo or video evidence?

Photo or video evidence can be included when it is part of the agreed inspection or warehouse task and site rules permit it. Evidence shows the observed sample or condition; it does not prove the status of every unit.

Do you inspect packaging and labeling?

Yes, when packaging and labeling checks are included in the scope. Checks may cover carton condition, product protection, quantities, marks, barcodes, labels, inserts, and packing configuration against the approved requirements.

What happens if products fail inspection?

We report the findings, separate confirmed facts from unresolved points, and request a supplier response or corrective-action plan. The client decides whether to rework, reinspect, accept an exception, reject goods, or take another contractual action.

Does inspection guarantee that every unit is defect-free?

No. Inspection reduces risk within the limits of the agreed method, sample, checklist, access, and timing. Laboratory testing, engineering validation, certification, and regulatory approval require appropriately qualified providers.

Consolidation

Receiving, checking, and preparing cargo

Can you receive goods from multiple suppliers?

Yes, subject to warehouse acceptance and an agreed receiving plan. Each supplier should use the required shipment reference, carton marks, packing list, and delivery information so incoming goods can be identified correctly.

What do you verify when cargo arrives at the warehouse?

Receiving checks can cover supplier identity, carton count, visible condition, marks, weight or dimensions, and matching against the provided packing list. Product-level counting or inspection must be separately defined.

Can you combine shipments from different factories?

Yes, where the products, schedules, documentation, packaging, and route are compatible. We coordinate receipt and cargo preparation, then confirm the proposed consolidated shipment before release.

Can you relabel or repack goods before export?

Potentially. Please provide the approved labels, placement instructions, packaging specification, quantities, and any destination or marketplace rules. Regulated or product-compliance labels must be reviewed by the responsible qualified party.

Can you assemble kits from components supplied by different factories?

Basic kitting may be coordinated when the bill of materials, quantities, work instructions, quality checks, packaging, and traceability requirements are clear. Complex assembly or testing needs a separate feasibility review.

How do you decide whether goods should ship together or separately?

We review cargo compatibility, readiness, size, destination, documentation, handling risk, and operational priorities. We then present the practical options; the client approves the shipment plan and any trade-offs.

International delivery

Freight, Incoterms, customs, and final mile

Which international shipping methods can you coordinate?

Depending on the cargo and route, options may include express, air freight, sea freight, rail, road, or a combined service. We assess the shipment details and explain which options are available rather than assuming one method fits every project.

What is the difference between DDP and DAP?

Under DDP, the seller carries the stated delivery obligations including import clearance and applicable import duties and taxes. Under DAP, the seller delivers to the named place, while the buyer handles import clearance and related import charges. The contract should name the exact place and state who acts as importer.

Is DDU still a current Incoterms rule?

No. DDU is a legacy term and is not an Incoterms 2020 rule. When a quotation uses DDU informally, ask for the responsibilities to be written clearly; DAP is commonly considered when the buyer handles import clearance and import charges.

Can you coordinate customs clearance and final-mile delivery?

Potentially, where a suitable route and service partner are available. The parties must confirm the shipper, consignee, importer, product classification, supporting documents, duties and taxes, customs-query process, and delivery-site limitations.

Which destination markets can you support?

Our priority enquiry markets are the United States, selected European markets, Canada, and Australia. Actual availability is confirmed for each product, origin, destination postal code, shipment profile, and required service.

Can cargo insurance be arranged?

Insurance options may be reviewed for eligible shipments. Coverage is not automatic; the insured value, goods, route, exclusions, packing requirements, claims evidence, and responsible policyholder must be confirmed before shipment.

Hardware startups

Supporting product teams without a China office

Can you support a hardware startup from samples to its first shipment?

Potentially. We can connect supplier options, sample rounds, production milestones, agreed quality checks, cargo preparation, and international delivery. Scope depends on the product’s maturity, suppliers, documentation, destination, and launch requirements.

Can you coordinate component and finished-goods suppliers?

Yes, when supplier dependencies, part references, ownership, delivery points, and acceptance checks are documented. Technical compatibility and engineering approval remain with the client or its qualified product team.

How do you handle engineering changes during production?

We can record the change request, affected documents, supplier response, inventory impact, and approval status. A revised specification or controlled instruction should be approved before the supplier implements a material change.

Can you support pilot runs or small initial batches?

Potentially, subject to supplier acceptance and a clear validation plan. The batch should have defined objectives, inspection criteria, issue tracking, and a release decision before scaling production.

How can you help when our team has no China sourcing office?

We can provide a China-side coordination point for supplier follow-up, samples, checks, cargo receiving, consolidation, and delivery handoffs. Your team still retains product ownership, technical decisions, legal approvals, and final commercial authority.

Can you manage product certification for a hardware launch?

We can help collect supplier documents and coordinate with nominated testing or compliance providers. Certification strategy, product classification, testing decisions, and market approval should be led by qualified specialists and the responsible seller or importer.

Special cargo

Batteries, sensitive goods, and oversized items

Can you ship products that contain batteries?

Potentially. We first review the battery type, configuration, rating, product and packing details, quantity, route, and available safety documents. Acceptance depends on accurate declaration, packaging, carrier rules, and destination requirements.

What documents may be needed for battery products?

Requirements vary by battery type, product configuration, mode, carrier, and route. Common requests may include battery specifications, safety test information, safety data, transport classification, packing details, and product photographs; the required set is confirmed after review.

Can you handle liquid, powder, magnetic, or other sensitive goods?

Selected products may be assessed, but acceptance is product- and route-specific. Provide the exact composition or technical description, use, packaging, quantity, photographs, and available safety or compliance documents before planning transport.

Can you coordinate oversized or heavy cargo?

Yes, subject to feasibility. Provide exact packed dimensions, gross weight, lifting points or center-of-gravity information where relevant, origin and destination access, loading conditions, and photographs. Special packing, equipment, permits, or site arrangements may be required.

Can you ship branded goods?

Potentially, after reviewing the brand, product, ownership, sales authorization, supplier relationship, and route requirements. We do not support counterfeit goods or shipments that lack required authorization or accurate documentation.

Are there goods you will not handle?

Yes. We do not support illegal goods, counterfeit goods, inaccurate declarations, duty avoidance, or cargo that cannot meet applicable safety, documentation, carrier, or destination requirements. Other exclusions are confirmed after product review.

Fulfillment & returns

Warehouse support after international delivery

Can you coordinate overseas warehousing and labeling?

Potentially, through suitable facilities for the destination and product. Services may include receiving, storage, labeling, repacking, and dispatch, but the warehouse, operating scope, product restrictions, and responsibilities must be confirmed first.

Can you support one-piece fulfillment or dropshipping?

Potentially, where a suitable warehouse and order process are available. We first review the destinations, product, packaging, order data, inventory controls, dispatch requirements, returns process, and expected operating pattern.

Can you prepare products for a marketplace or retail channel?

We can coordinate agreed labeling, barcode, bundling, insert, carton, or routing-guide tasks when clear instructions are provided. The client remains responsible for confirming the current marketplace, retailer, product, and regulatory requirements.

Can you process customer returns?

Return handling may include receiving, identification, basic condition checks, photography, restocking, consolidation, forwarding, or disposal under an approved instruction. Availability depends on the country, warehouse, product, and agreed process.

Can returned products be inspected or reworked?

Basic inspection, sorting, or simple rework may be possible if the criteria, work instruction, parts, tools, safety controls, and disposition rules are clear. Technical repair or regulated work requires an appropriately qualified provider.

Can fulfillment data connect to our sales system?

Integration depends on the selected warehouse and the systems involved. We can document the required order and inventory data, then confirm whether an API, platform connector, file exchange, or manual process is available.

Commercial process

Fit, scope, and getting started

Who is Jinghang Global Supply Chain designed to support?

We focus on overseas hardware startups, brands, e-commerce businesses, and small to medium-sized companies without an in-house China sourcing team. We are especially useful when supplier, quality, warehouse, and delivery handoffs need one coordinated workflow.

Who may not be a good fit?

We may not be suitable for enquiries based only on the lowest freight quote, requests without enough product or shipment information to assess, or goods involving non-compliance or inaccurate customs declarations. We prioritize workable scopes and transparent responsibilities.

What information do you need for an initial project review?

Please provide the product and intended use, specifications or photos, supplier status and locations, order stage, quantity, packed dimensions and weight, cargo value, sensitive attributes, available documents, destination postal code, and support required.

How are responsibilities defined for a project?

Before work begins, the scope should identify the parties, deliverables, client approvals, supplier responsibilities, third-party services, information required, exclusions, cargo release authority, and exception process. Material changes should be confirmed in writing.

How is the service structure determined?

The structure depends on the required coordination, supplier work, inspection, warehouse handling, transport, customs-related support, fulfillment, and third-party services. We separate included tasks, assumptions, exclusions, and pass-through items in the written proposal or quotation.

What happens after I submit a project brief?

We review the product, project stage, supplier situation, cargo, destination, and support requested. We then identify missing information and confirm the next practical step, which may be clarification, feasibility review, a proposal, or a quotation.
