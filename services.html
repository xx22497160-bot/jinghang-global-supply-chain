01 · Source

Supplier Sourcing & Factory Coordination

Turn a product requirement into a workable supplier search while your team retains control of the final commercial decision.

Start a supplier search

Finding a supplier is only the beginning.

We help clarify the brief, identify potential manufacturers, coordinate initial conversations, organize quotations and samples, and keep communication moving through the early decision process.

  • Clarify product, material, process, volume, and quality requirements
  • Search for potential manufacturers or suitable suppliers
  • Collect and organize initial supplier information
  • Coordinate quotations and commercial questions
  • Arrange sample requests and comparison
  • Follow up on production-related communication
  • Escalate unclear requirements, delays, or exceptions
  • Coordinate visits or third-party checks where agreed
Scope boundary Supplier screening reduces uncertainty but cannot remove all commercial, technical, intellectual-property, or financial risk. Final supplier approval and contracting remain with the client unless a different written scope is agreed.

02 · Prepare

Sample & Procurement Support

Keep the many small but critical tasks around samples and early orders organized before larger commitments are made.

Coordinate samples or an order

Make early-stage decisions with better information.

We help coordinate versions, supplier questions, packing requirements, milestones, and sample consolidation so your team can compare what arrived with what was requested.

  • Coordinate sample orders from one or multiple suppliers
  • Confirm available versions, colors, accessories, and packing details
  • Consolidate samples for one international shipment
  • Organize quotations and order information
  • Follow up on agreed purchasing milestones
  • Coordinate supplier questions and exceptions
  • Maintain an agreed record of key requirements
  • Prepare the handoff to quality checks or production follow-up
Scope boundary Payment flow, ownership of goods, supplier contracts, and purchasing authority must be confirmed before work begins. Jinghang coordinates only the responsibilities stated in the agreed scope.

03 · Verify

Quality Control & Inspection

Identify quality, quantity, labeling, and packing issues before goods are released into the international logistics chain.

Plan an inspection

Problems are easier to address before cargo leaves China.

Based on an agreed checklist and inspection scope, we coordinate product checks, document findings, and help communicate corrective actions before release.

  • Requirement and checklist preparation
  • Comparison with a confirmed reference sample where available
  • Pre-production, during-production, or pre-shipment checks
  • Quantity, workmanship, function, and appearance checks
  • Labeling and packaging review
  • Photo and video evidence
  • Defect and exception reporting
  • Corrective-action and reinspection coordination
Scope boundary Inspection is limited by the agreed checklist, sample size, timing, and access conditions. It does not replace laboratory testing, product certification, engineering validation, or regulatory approval.

04 · Move

Consolidation & International Delivery

Connect warehouse receiving, cargo preparation, international transport, customs responsibilities, and final delivery.

Plan a shipment

The logistics problem starts before cargo reaches the port.

When several suppliers, packaging formats, and deadlines are involved, we coordinate the pre-shipment handoffs as well as the international movement.

  • Receive cargo from one or multiple Chinese suppliers
  • Match cartons and quantities against available documents
  • Coordinate labeling, repacking, palletizing, or protection
  • Combine shipments where operationally appropriate
  • Compare suitable air, sea, express, or multimodal options
  • Coordinate export documentation
  • Coordinate DDP or DAP delivery arrangements where available
  • Coordinate customs information, milestones, and final delivery
Scope boundary Route availability, customs handling, duties, taxes, transit estimates, and delivery scope depend on the product, documents, origin, destination, and confirmed quotation. Responsibilities must be stated in writing before shipment.

05 · Solve

Battery, Sensitive & Oversized Cargo

Assess product details, documentation, packaging, route, and handling before confirming a feasible plan.

Request a cargo assessment

Non-standard products need more than a standard quote.

Products with batteries, sensitive characteristics, unusual dimensions, or special handling requirements need the right documentation, packaging, route, and service partners.

  • Built-in, standalone, and product-packed batteries
  • Magnetic, liquid, powder, chemical, or food-contact characteristics
  • Branded products requiring authorization evidence
  • Oversized, overweight, long, fragile, or irregular cargo
  • Product and safety-document review
  • Packaging and handling requirements
  • Route and carrier feasibility
  • Destination-specific restrictions and exclusions
Scope boundary Acceptance is never automatic. It depends on accurate product information, supporting documents, packaging, route rules, carrier requirements, and destination restrictions.

06 · Fulfill

Overseas Warehouse, Fulfillment & Returns

Coordinate selected post-arrival activities around the destination market, sales channel, volume, and return workflow.

Discuss fulfillment requirements

Support can continue after international arrival.

For businesses that need more than port-to-door transport, we can coordinate selected overseas warehouse and fulfillment activities through suitable service arrangements.

  • Warehouse receiving and basic inventory handling
  • Carton or unit labeling
  • Marketplace or retailer preparation
  • Repacking and kitting
  • Pick-and-pack and direct-to-customer shipping
  • Dropshipping and order dispatch
  • Return receiving and basic condition checks
  • Restocking, forwarding, consolidation, or disposal
Scope boundary Warehouse locations, cut-off times, storage terms, integrations, return rules, and product restrictions must be confirmed for each market and service arrangement.
