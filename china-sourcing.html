Sourcing support at a glance

A documented comparison, not an unexplained factory recommendation.

Jinghang uses the agreed product and commercial brief to search available supplier channels, collect available information, compare candidates, flag gaps, and prepare the client's next decision.

China-side sourcing support without a full local purchasing team.

02 · Build the brief

Clear requirements produce more comparable supplier responses.

Missing details can be flagged, but supplier search cannot replace product design, engineering, compliance, or commercial decisions.

Information to prepare

  • Product name, intended use, preferred production area, materials, specifications, dimensions, finish, drawings, photos, links, or an approved sample where available
  • Features, accessories, packaging, labeling, branding, or customization requirements
  • Expected order quantity, repeat-volume estimate, sample requirement, target price or budget range when relevant, timing, destination, and project stage
  • Target market and known testing, certification, labeling, packaging, or regulatory requirements
  • Battery, liquid, powder, magnet, branded-product, or other regulated characteristics
  • The comparison factors your team wants prioritized
Early-idea boundary Jinghang may identify missing sourcing inputs. Industrial design, engineering validation, patent or intellectual-property advice, laboratory testing, certification, and regulatory approval are not included in preliminary supplier sourcing.

03 · Search and screen

A requirement-led search, not a list chosen by price alone.

Potential candidates are compared on the same agreed basis and unresolved points stay visible.

How preliminary sourcing works

  1. Confirm the product brief and comparison criteria.
  2. Search available supplier channels and relevant production areas.
  3. Collect available public and supplier-provided information.
  4. Compare requirement fit, MOQ, quotation basis, sample details, stated timing, packaging, customization, and communication.
  5. Flag missing, unclear, or inconsistent information rather than treating it as verified.
  6. Obtain the client's decision on quotation clarification, samples, a video call, separately scoped checks, or no further action.
Screening boundary Preliminary screening is not a factory audit, compliance certification, financial due diligence, legal opinion, engineering validation, or product-quality guarantee. Supplier statements and documents remain supplier-provided unless independent verification is separately arranged.

04 · Compare

Receive evidence, open questions, and next actions.

A missing field is shown as missing rather than assumed.

Standard sourcing deliverables

  • Confirmed sourcing brief and comparison criteria
  • Candidate-supplier comparison table
  • Available quotations, supplier-provided documents, photographs, links, or sample information
  • Open-issues, risk-notes, and next-actions list
  • Sample comparison or inspection records only when separately included

Typical comparison fields

Supplier name and location; product, material, specification, and customization fit; available business information; MOQ; quotation basis; sample availability and cost; supplier-stated timing; packaging and labeling; supplier-provided compliance documents; communication notes; inconsistencies; known risks; and questions requiring a client decision.

05 · Decide

Regular updates with written client approval before commitment.

During active sourcing, Jinghang normally provides one consolidated update every two to three business days.

Decision-point workflow

  1. The client confirms the brief and comparison criteria.
  2. Jinghang conducts preliminary search, communication, and comparison within scope.
  3. The client selects candidates for clarification, samples, or separately scoped deeper checks.
  4. Jinghang coordinates approved next steps and updates the comparison.
  5. The client gives final supplier approval in writing before an applicable purchase, payment, or production instruction.
Client responsibilities Final supplier selection, contracting, product specifications, compliance, intellectual property, payment authorization, and commercial decisions remain with the client. A different update cadence may be agreed in writing; the standard cadence is not a completion-time promise.

06 · Connect

Move a client-approved supplier into the next agreed stage.

Supplier approval is a decision point, not the end of the supply chain.

Connected coordination may include

  • Quotation questions, sample versions, specification changes, and supplier responses
  • Compatible sample consolidation from several suppliers
  • Pre-shipment inspection against an agreed method, checklist, and evidence scope
  • Multi-supplier consolidation after readiness, compatibility, packaging, and destination review
  • Express, air, sea, DDP, or DAP review against cargo, documents, origin, destination, and customs requirements

Direct answers

China sourcing questions

Answers reflect a preliminary-screening and client-approval model.

What information do you need before starting a supplier search?

Provide the product name and use, preferred production area if any, materials, specifications or references, expected quantity, sample requirement, target market, known compliance or packaging needs, timing, destination, target price or budget range when price is a comparison factor, and comparison priorities. Missing information is flagged rather than assumed.

What will I receive from a supplier sourcing project?

Standard deliverables are a confirmed sourcing brief, candidate-supplier comparison table, available supplier quotations or materials, and an open-issues and next-actions list. Sample comparison or inspection records are included only when those activities are separately agreed.

Can Jinghang help me find a reliable supplier in China?

Jinghang can identify potential candidates, collect available information, coordinate questions and samples, and compare suppliers against written requirements. Preliminary screening reduces uncertainty but does not prove future reliability, compliance, financial strength, or product quality. The client makes the final supplier decision.

Can you verify whether a supplier is a real factory?

Preliminary screening can include available business and location information plus supplier-provided capability information. A video call, site visit, professional audit, or independent verification may be reviewed as a separate scope. Preliminary screening alone is not proof that a supplier is a manufacturer or can meet every requirement.

How do you compare potential suppliers?

Candidates are compared against agreed factors such as product and material fit, production location, available business information, MOQ, quotation basis, samples, stated lead time, packaging or customization, communication, missing information, and open risks.

Can you coordinate suppliers that I already found?

Yes. Jinghang can coordinate requirements, quotation or specification questions, samples, agreed follow-up, inspection preparation, collection, and delivery handoffs with existing suppliers. Responsibilities and decision points are confirmed in the written scope.

Can you manage sourcing, samples, quality inspection, and shipping together?

Yes, these stages can be connected through one agreed coordination workflow. Each stage has its own information requirements, execution party, approval point, commercial terms, and limitations, which are confirmed before work begins.

How long does it take to find a supplier in China?

There is no universal fixed sourcing time. Timing depends on product complexity, brief completeness, supplier response, sample requirements, requested checks, and client decision speed. Consolidated updates are normally provided every two to three business days during active sourcing, but that cadence is not a completion promise.
